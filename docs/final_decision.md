# Final Decision: paymentChange

## Summary

We tested a subscription price decrease from 400 to 160 for new users.

The goal was to understand whether a lower price would increase conversion without hurting revenue too much.

---

## What happened

- Conversion increased significantly  
- The difference in conversion is statistically significant  
- ARPU decreased  
- The ARPU difference is not statistically significant  

---

## How to interpret this

The experiment clearly shows that lowering the price works in terms of conversion.

This is expected — a lower price reduces the barrier to purchase, so more users are willing to subscribe.

However, there is a downside.

Even though more users convert, we are earning less revenue per user.  
And while this drop in ARPU is not statistically significant, the size of the decrease is large enough to be concerning.

So we end up in a classic situation:

- more users are paying  
- but each user приносит меньше денег  

---

## Decision

Do not roll out the change yet.

---

## Why

- The experiment successfully improves conversion  
- But there is a real risk of losing revenue  
- The data does not clearly confirm that ARPU drop is stable  
- The uncertainty is too high to make a safe product decision  

In simple terms:  
the idea works, but it may be too expensive for the business.

---

## What to do next

- Run the experiment longer  
- Check ARPU stability on a larger sample  
- Look at segments (maybe some users behave differently)  
- Evaluate longer-term effects (retention, LTV)

---

## Final note

Price reduction is a strong lever for growth, but it directly affects monetization.

Before rolling it out, we need to be sure that we are not trading long-term revenue for short-term conversion gains.
